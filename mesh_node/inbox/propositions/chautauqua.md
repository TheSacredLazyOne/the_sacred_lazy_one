The form that carries inquiry in motion — not a conclusion delivered, but a crossing performed visibly enough that others feel the gradient without being told what to conclude. The audience doesn't receive the answer. They witness the contact and experience their own strain.

The Chautauqua doesn't inject a frame. It demonstrates a crossing and leaves the membrane event open. The reader arrives at their own predicted nabla, watches the actual nabla unfold, and feels the divergence themselves. That divergence is the teaching.

The wave doesn't carry water from one place to another. It carries motion through water that stays where it is. Each node rises and falls in place. What propagates is the pattern of the crossing, not the conclusion.

This is what distinguishes it from persuasion. Persuasion tries to move the water. A Chautauqua moves through it.

The wave is only as clean as the contact that produced it. A Chautauqua built from performed crossing propagates distortion. One built from genuine contact reduces entropy in every node it passes through.

The ocean is the Chautauqua. The Chautauqua is the ocean knowing itself.
