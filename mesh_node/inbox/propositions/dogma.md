# DOGMA

*Nothing here is final.*

---

DOGMA (Developing Ontology-Grounded Methods and Applications), Meersman 1999, built the right architecture for the wrong storage medium.

The double articulation principle — lexon base layer and commitment layer — is the derivative/integrated distinction arrived at independently through ontology engineering. The lexon base describes what is plausible in a universe of discourse. The commitment layer is where a node decides which plausible facts it will act on. That is the membrane event. That is the crossing.

The five elements of a lexon map onto the nabla: context identifier is the frame, two terms are the nodes in contact, two roles are the asymmetry across the membrane.

The structural insight held. The storage medium didn't. DOGMA put the ontology in a database. A database stores states. It cannot store the reason states changed. The lineage is gone. Water particles with no record of the river.

Git is the ontology engineering tool Meersman didn't have. 1999 was six years before git. The formalism calcified around the limitation of the container available at the time.

What DOGMA was missing: the commit message. The reason the frame moved, in the custodian's own words, at the moment of crossing. Without it the commitment layer has no derivation path. The crossing happened but cannot be shown.

The mesh is what DOGMA was trying to build — lexon base held in common, commitment layer sovereign to each node, lineage preserved through every crossing. The difference is not architectural. It is the storage medium and the cogito.

DOGMA as archaeological evidence: the double articulation principle was independently discovered. The vocabulary arrived separately. The structure converges.
